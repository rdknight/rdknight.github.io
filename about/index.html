A Short Career Bio

A Bakersfield, California boy.

I've worked on farms and packing sheds in the summer, for 5 years I fought forest and grassland fires in the southern mountains, was a gas jockey a few times, worked on oil production rigs on the coast, installed automotive components at Sears, and operated mainframe computers during the swing/graveyard shifts.

After finishing school (Bakersfield Jr. College, CSU Bakersfield, UC Santa Barbara), I was hired by Bell Labs in Colorado. Essentially I was sucked into the burgeoning telecom business - my math degrees became a despressingly fogging past. From AT&T, I jump to Qualcomm, and then got devoured by Ericsson.

Then, in 2002, I co-created a synthetic black hole by founding RoadNarrows LLC. The dream was to create intelligent robots and software and live a decent life. We were idea rich but cash poor.

Today RoadNarrows simply sales third party robots and related hardware along with first tier technical support. Through RN contacts, I still get an occasional SW gig.

Today, I research AI on my own. Always looking for work.
