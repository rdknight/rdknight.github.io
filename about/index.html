A Short Career Bio

A Bakersfield, California boy.

I've worked on farms and packing sheds in the summer. For 5 years I fought forest and grassland fires in the southern mountains. I was a gas jockey a few times, enjoying the lead filling my lungs. Tried my hand on oil production rigs on the coast. Installed automotive components at Sears. Operated mainframe computers during the swing/graveyard shifts.

After finishing school (Bakersfield Jr. College, CSU Bakersfield, UC Santa Barbara), I was hired by Bell Labs in Colorado. Essentially I was sucked into the burgeoning telecom business, my math degrees became a despressingly foggy past. From AT&T, I jump to Qualcomm, and then got devoured by Ericsson.

Then, in 2002, I co-created a synthetic black hole by founding RoadNarrows LLC. The dream was to create intelligent robots and software and live a decent life. We were idea rich but cash poor. And more cashed poor, ...

Today RoadNarrows simply sales third party robots and related hardware, along with first tier technical support. Through RN contacts, I still get an occasional SW gig.

Today, I research AI on my own. Always looking for work.
